Label only completed waves (not mid-swing). Place the label at the exact bar that ends the move ‚Äî precision avoids cumulative errors later. Think in terms of the Box Method (Chapter 1) ‚Äî label a wave when its box is complete.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Core Rules of Impulse Waves

Three inviolate rules ‚Äî if any are violated, the count is invalid (possible diagonal or corrective structure instead):

  • Rule 1 ‚Äî Wave 3 can never be the shortest of waves 1, 3, 5.
  • Rule 2 ‚Äî Wave 4 cannot overlap into wave 1 territory.
  • Rule 3 ‚Äî Wave 2 cannot retrace beyond the start of wave 1.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Guidelines (Not Absolute Rules)

Guidelines are strong tendencies:

  • Alternation: If wave 2 is deep/complex, wave 4 will likely be shallow/simple.
  • Equality: Wave 5 often approximates the length of wave 1.
  • Volume: Volume typically peaks on wave 3 and wanes by wave 5 (look for divergence).
  • Time symmetry: Wave 1‚Äôs time often mirrors wave 5's duration.
